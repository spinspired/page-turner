I The idea

A curl, not a rotation

Why this rarely exists

CSS transforms are affine — a plane can turn, but it cannot bend. So this page is not a plane: it is strips hinged into a kinematic chain. Each hinge adds the same small angle, and together they form a circular arc. The top and bottom edges curve, and because every strip carries its own clipped slice of the content, the type bends with the paper.

Note

Look at this page mid-turn: the edges are arcs, not lines — and this very sentence is following the bend.

a plane can’t bend. so don’t use one.
II The anchor

The spine never moves

Every transform is rooted at the binding

The base rotation turns around the spine — and every strip's transform is anchored there too, so the spine is the rotation axis itself and stays put to the pixel. The hinges between the strips exist only as mathematics: computed positions along the arc, not nested elements.

Note

Anything anchored on the axis cannot drift. Anything scaled by sin(π·g) must land flat. Correctness by geometry, not by choreography.

the binding is a promise.
III The layers

Three layers, three seam strategies

Why there are no hairlines between the strips

Behind the content sits a color-matched paper base, overlapping generously — whatever peeks through a wedge gap is indistinguishable from the page. The content overlaps 1.2px to the right only: real continuation, covering the front seams. And the curl light is no layer at all — it is a brightness filter painted into the surfaces, so there is nothing left to compose. Backface culling picks the visible side per strip, exactly, on the compositor.

Note

The most elegant seam strategy turned out to be: don’t be a surface in the first place.

every layer earns its overlap.
IV The physics

It knows which way you are going

Direction-aware, with a soft swing-through

Paper always bends against the motion — the free edge trails behind. Scroll back and the page bows the other way. Reverse mid-flight and the curl doesn’t snap: it eases through flat to the opposite side, the way real paper springs when you change your mind halfway through a page.

Note

Try it here: wobble up and down while this page stands upright, and watch the bow swing through.

wobble me. i can take it.
V Your turn

Make it yours

Everything is a knob or a copy-paste

The knobs at the top of script.js: SEGMENTS (smoothness), CURL (drama), SHADING (light), and SCROLL_PER_FLIP (pace), and PERSPECTIVE (camera). Adding a page is adding one <article class="page"> — scroll length, ribbon and controls adapt on their own. Small screens and reduced-motion readers get the same book as a calm, static stack.

Note

Fork it, bend it, break it — the spine will hold.
